# Cleland Studio: Design System & UI/UX Guidelines

## Naming & Purpose
This document serves as the **Design Authority** for all components within the Cleland Studio ecosystem, particularly the Blueprint Configurator. It is built upon the `ui-ux-pro-max` intelligence standards, tailored specifically to the "Executive Editorial" and "Cinematic Dark Mode" brand identity.

---

## 1. Accessibility (CRITICAL - Priority 1)

*   **Color Contrast (`color-contrast`)**: All text must meet a minimum 4.5:1 contrast ratio against its background. The primary foreground (`hsl(45 10% 92%)`) against the void background (`hsl(220 15% 4%)`) well exceeds this. Be careful when applying text over the `accent` (gold) color; dark text (`#090A0C`) must be used for readability.
*   **Focus States (`focus-states`)**: Interactive elements must retain visible focus rings. Use the brand's amber/gold accent for `ring-accent` to provide clear, premium keyboard navigation.
*   **Semantic HTML**: Rely on proper `<button>`, `<a>`, `<nav>`, and `<main>` tags.
*   **Motion Reduction (`reduced-motion`)**: The `typography.css` globally forces zero-duration animations if the user has `prefers-reduced-motion` enabled. Ensure no critical functionality relies solely on an animation finishing.

## 2. Touch & Interaction (CRITICAL - Priority 2)

*   **Touch Target Size (`touch-target-size`)**: All clickable elements (buttons, form inputs, toggles, icons) must have a minimum touch target of 44x44px, especially critical on mobile viewports for the Configurator.
*   **Hover States (`hover-vs-tap`)**: Do not cause layout shifts on hover. Use color, opacity, and subtle box-shadow transitions instead of scaling elements in ways that shift siblings. (e.g., the `.work-card` scales internally using `overflow-hidden`).
*   **Cursors (`cursor-pointer`)**: Ensure `cursor-pointer` is explicitly applied to all clickable cards and custom interactive elements.
*   **Disabled States (`loading-buttons`)**: Buttons triggering async operations must visually dim and disable pointer events to prevent double-submissions.

## 3. Layout, Spacing, & Rhythm (HIGH - Priority 4)

*   **Rhythm Tokens**: Adhere strictly to the defined CSS variables for vertical rhythm:
    *   `--space-section: 2.5rem` (40px) - Between major regions.
    *   `--space-group: 1.5rem` (24px) - Between related items.
    *   `--space-element: 0.75rem` (12px) - Between tightly coupled elements.
*   **Z-Index Management (`z-index-management`)**: Keep z-indexes standardized:
    *   `0`: Atmosphere / Background gradients
    *   `10`: Base Structural Background
    *   `20`: Structural Elements (grids, dividers)
    *   `30`: Typography / Content
    *   `40`: Foreground UI (buttons, cards)
    *   `50`: Overlays / Modals / Navbars
*   **Scroll Engine**: The app utilizes Lenis for momentum scrolling. Do not fight the scroll-hijacking container. Apply `data-lenis-prevent` to internal scrolling containers (like sidebars or long dropdowns) to prevent overscroll chaining.

## 4. Typography & Layout (MEDIUM - Priority 5)

*   **Hierarchy Strictness**: Never mix font families outside their designated roles.
    *   `Syne`: Strictly for uppercase, tracked-out structural labels, section headers, and massive numbers.
    *   `Domaine Display Narrow / Cormorant`: Strictly for expressive, editorial headings and narrative blocks.
    *   `Raela Pro`: Strictly for body copy, paragraphs, button text, and standard UI.
*   **Line-Height & Width**:
    *   Body text should maintain a line-height of `1.5` to `1.75`.
    *   Editorial Headings should drop down to `1.2` or even `0.9` for massive heroes.
    *   Constrain line-lengths (max-width `prose` or `max-w-3xl`) for readability.
*   **Responsive Typography**: Mobile breakpoints must tighten line-heights slightly (defined in `typography.css`) to prevent text from feeling disconnected.

## 5. Visual Polish & Theming (MEDIUM)

*   **No Emojis**: Do not use emojis in the UI. Rely exclusively on SVG iconography (e.g., Lucide, Heroicons, or Radix Icons) styled with the current text color (`currentColor`).
*   **Glassmorphism & Gradients**: When creating floating cards or headers, use base colors with opacity (e.g., `bg-card/80`) backed by a `backdrop-blur-sm` or `backdrop-blur-md`. This ensures the atmospheric gradients glow through the UI.
*   **Borders**: Prefer very subtle borders (`border-border/50` or `1px solid hsl(var(--border) / 0.3)`) to cleanly delineate cards rather than relying heavily on drop-shadows in dark mode.
*   **Cinematic Easing**: Force all transitions to use the custom `--ease-cinematic` (`cubic-bezier(0.22, 1, 0.36, 1)`) rather than default linear or ease-in-out curves for a more premium feel.
