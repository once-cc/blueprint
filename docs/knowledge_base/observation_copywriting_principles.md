# Observation Engine: Copywriting Principles

> Companion to **@value_equation.md**. Governs the editorial voice of all automated Blueprint correspondence.

---

## Core Rule

**Interpret, never relay.** The user already knows what they selected. Our job is to tell them what it *means* — something they couldn't have concluded on their own.

| ❌ Relay | ✅ Interpretation |
|:---|:---|
| "Your conversion goal is Call Booking" | "Your portfolio has a job — earn the click before the CTA appears" |
| "Your brand voice is Formal + Bold" | "You want authority without avant-garde. That's a very specific design lane." |
| "Your risk tolerance is Cautious" | "You want to push boundaries, but only ones you can see the other side of" |

---

## The 7 Principles

### 1. One Insight Per Email

Every nurture email carries exactly one observation. Short, declarative sentences. 5–7 sentences max. If a sentence doesn't change how the reader thinks, cut it.

### 2. Damaging Admission

Open from a position of honesty, not perfection. Admitting what you don't do builds trust in what you claim to see.

> "Most studios would look at your Blueprint and build a quote. We read it differently."

### 3. The Hedge

"Correct me if I'm wrong" language does three things:
- Softens interpretation (accuracy wiggle room)
- Signals confidence disguised as humility  
- **Invites a reply** (highest-value email engagement)

> "Correct me if I'm wrong, but your brand wants to feel like a members-only lounge — not a marketplace."

Use sparingly — once per sequence, not every email.

### 4. Value Equation Lens

Frame observations through the four levers (never name them explicitly):

| Lever | Email Voice |
|:---|:---|
| **Dream Outcome** | "You want calls, not clicks" |
| **Likelihood** | "Your portfolio sequence can make the booking feel inevitable" |
| **Time Delay** | "A 4-week timeline means the design system does the heavy lifting" |
| **Effort** | "Gated content means you don't chase — they come back" |

### 5. Open Loops, Not Summaries

Never close an email by restating what you just said. End with an implication of more:

- "We're still reading your Blueprint. More soon."
- "There's something else in your scope we haven't mentioned yet."
- "One last thing before we go quiet."

### 6. Mirror Their Language

The `ctaPrimaryLabel` field contains their exact words. Using it in copy creates a "they actually read mine" moment. Same for `salesPersonality` — echo the self-image:

| Personality | Mirror Words |
|:---|:---|
| `luxury_gatekeeper` | selective, invitation-only, curated, earned |
| `fast_freemium` | zero friction, instant, no barrier, try-first |
| `quietly_authoritative` | understated, let-the-work-speak, confident |
| `educator` | depth, teach, value-first, long game |
| `slow_burn_strategist` | nurture, editorial, trust-over-time |

### 7. Progressive Revelation

The sequence should feel like someone is progressively studying the submission. Each email builds on the last — the reader should feel the analysis is deepening, not repeating.

| Email | Feeling |
|:---|:---|
| 1 (Immediate) | "They mapped what I chose" |
| 2 (1hr) | "They noticed something specific" |
| 3 (24hr) | "They read between the lines" |
| 4 (72hr) | "They see the strategic picture" |
| 5 (7d) | "They know what to do — I should talk to them" |

## Structural Rules

- **Subject lines**: Short, specific, no clickbait. State what the email is about, not what it sells.
- **Sender**: "Cleland Studio" — not a person's name. The persona is the studio, not an individual.
- **Signoff**: "— Cleland Studio" — dash, not "Regards" or "Best."
- **No images, no logos, no HTML complexity** in nurture emails. Plain editorial. The Blueprint PDF was the artifact; the emails are the conversation.
- **CTA only in Email 5**. Emails 2–4 have no call-to-action. Pure insight. This builds the deprivation mechanic.
