# The Value Equation

(Dream Outcome × Perceived Likelihood of Achievement)
÷
(Time Delay × Effort & Sacrifice)
= Value

---

## What does this mean?

Value..

In business, value is not what your product is.
Value is what your product does for the customer — emotionally, practically, and socially.

Value is not defined by you.

Value is defined entirely by the customer's perception:
"How much does this help me get what I want…
with the least risk, effort, time, and uncertainty?"

You can think of value as:
Value = The gap between where they are now and where they want to be…
AND how quickly, easily, and safely you can get them there.

The bigger that gap, the more valuable the solution.
The faster and easier the journey, the more valuable the solution.
The less risk involved, the more valuable the solution.

This is why Hormozi created the Value Equation — to show that value is not a static thing but a ratio made of psychological levers.

Value = Desired Outcome / Perceived Difficulty

At the simplest level, value is:
Value = The result they want
÷
The resistance they feel.

If resistance drops → value skyrockets.
If desire grows → value skyrockets.

That's why value is manipulatable.

# 1. Dream Outcome

The First Component to creating value

## What is a dream outcome

- Four layers to understand it
- Examples
- Questions to identify it
- How dream outcome impacts your offer

### What is a dream outcome?

The Dream Outcome is the ideal, perfect result your customer wishes they could get — the outcome that would fundamentally change their life, business, or identity.

It answers the single most important question:
"How much does the customer want the thing?"

The stronger the desire,
the bigger the emotional pull,
the more valuable your offer becomes — before you've even delivered anything.

---

## The 4 Layers of a Dream Outcome

Most businesses only look at the surface, but high-level offer creation requires you to understand all four layers:

### Layer 1 — The Functional Result

This is the logical, practical outcome.

**Examples:**

- "A website that converts."
- "Clear skin without steroid creams."
- "Lose 5kg."
- "More leads."
- "A balm that stops eczema flare-ups."

This is what they say they want.
But this is the weakest layer.

### Layer 2 — The Emotional Result

This is what they truly want.

**Examples:**

- "To finally feel confident showing my business online."
- "To feel in control of my health."
- "To not worry about my baby's skin."
- "To stop feeling embarrassed."
- "To feel like a real business owner."
- "To feel attractive again."

This is where desire becomes buying power.

### Layer 3 — The Identity Transformation

This is the deepest layer — the "Who I become" shift.

**Examples:**

- "I am someone with a premium brand."
- "I am a confident mum who knows what works for my kids."
- "I am the sort of man who takes care of himself."
- "I am a successful founder."
- "I am a person who chooses natural solutions."
- "I am not struggling anymore."

Identity shifts are buy-now drivers.

### Layer 4 — The Social Outcome

This is how others will see them.

**Examples:**

- "People will see my brand and think I'm legit."
- "People won't judge my child's eczema anymore."
- "People will see how good my skin looks."
- "My friends will ask where I got this from."
- "Competitors will respect me."
- "My partner will notice the change."

People buy for private reasons,
but they justify it with public outcomes.

---

## The Dream Outcome = All Four Layers Combined

A high-value offer doesn't just promise a result…

It promises a new emotional reality,
a new social reality,
and a new identity.

When your offer aligns with all four, desire becomes magnetic.

---

## How to Identify the Dream Outcome

(3 simple questions)

Ask yourself:

1. What would completely change their situation?
   (Not slightly improve it.)

2. What do they fantasize about when nobody is watching?
   (Their real desire.)

3. Who do they want to become by achieving this?
   (the identity layer)

When you can answer these clearly → your Dream Outcome is dialled in.

---

## How Dream Outcome Impacts Your Offer

Increasing the Dream Outcome increases the numerator of the value equation.

- Make the result bigger
- Make the result more meaningful
- Make the result more emotional
- Make the result tied to identity
- Make the result socially significant
- Make the result something they deeply crave

This automatically makes your offer more valuable without changing delivery.

**Examples to show you the difference:**

Weak Dream Outcome (surface-level):
"Get a website built."

Strong Dream Outcome (full 4 layers):
"Launch a premium, category-of-one brand that finally represents your true value, gives you confidence, and attracts clients automatically — so you can step into the identity of a successful, respected business owner."

---

## Summary

Dream Outcome = The result they most want
\+ the emotion they attach to it
\+ who they become once they get it.

# 2. Perceived Likelihood of Achievement (PLA)

The Second Component to creating value

## Perceived Likelihood of Achievement (PLA)

…is the customer's internal belief that your product, service, or offer will actually work for them specifically.

### The key is..

People don't buy when they understand you.
They buy when they feel you understand them.

PLA is the difference between someone who loves your offer…
and someone who buys it.

### The Core Question PLA Answers

"Do I believe this will work for me?"

Not:
"Does it work in general?"
"Does it work for other people?"
"Does the seller think it works?"

The buyer is evaluating:

- My situation
- My past failures
- My limits
- My time
- My family context
- My budget
- My habits

Will this work given all of THAT?

If the answer becomes YES, conversion skyrockets.

---

## The Five PLA Drivers

PLA is a composite of five psychological sources of trust

### 1. Proof

This is the backbone.

Proof is evidence that:

- Others like them got results
- The offer has worked in the real world
- The seller delivered consistently

But the key word is **like them**.

People don't relate to abstract success.
They relate to mirrors of themselves.

**Strong PLA Proof includes:**

- Stories from similar demographics
- Stories from similar starting problems
- Simple results ("3 days later my eczema calmed down")
- Screenshots
- Short video testimonials
- Social proof (comments, shares, reviews)
- Proof of consistency ("We've fulfilled 1738 orders this year")

**Weak PLA Proof is:**

- General statements
- Unrelated industries
- High-level claims with no evidence

Proof doesn't need to be fancy — it needs to be relevant.

### 2. Guarantees

Guarantees reduce the buyer's fear of loss and increase PLA massively.

**Why?**

Because guarantees say:
"We're so confident this works,
we're taking on the risk — not you."

**There are different types:**

1. Basic Guarantees
2. Money-Back Guarantees
3. Satisfaction Guarantees
4. Performance Guarantees
5. Risk-Reversal Guarantees

**1. Basic Guarantees**

Examples:
- "If you don't feel relief in 3 days, contact us."
- "Risk-free trial."

Simple assurances that reduce fear and increase confidence.

**2. Money-Back Guarantees**

Example:
- "If your website draft doesn't arrive in 72 hours, we credit you X."

A direct financial safety net.

**3. Satisfaction Guarantees**

Example:
- "If you're not satisfied, we'll fix it for free."

Focused on customer happiness rather than strict performance.

**4. Performance Guarantees**

Example:
- "If this doesn't improve your skin within 7 days, we'll replace it."

Promises tied to specific, measurable outcomes.

**5. Risk-Reversal Guarantees**

Example:
- "You keep the product even if you refund."

Eliminate all downside by shifting risk from customer to business.

**Remember:**
Guarantees don't have to be financial.

Their purpose is simply to tell the customer:
"You're safe with us."

### 3. Experience & Expertise

This is your track record.

People want to know:

- "Have you done this before?"
- "Do you know what you're doing?"
- "Do you have skills I don't?"

The more niche the expertise, the stronger the PLA.

**Examples:**

- "We've built 48 websites across NZ this year."
- "Our balm is made using traditional Māori rongoā methods."
- "Our founders have 10 years in natural product formulation."
- "We sold out our last batch in 3 days."

You don't need to brag — you just need to show evidence of competency.

### 4. Process Clarity

This is the most overlooked PLA lever.

When people understand exactly how they will get the result, they feel safe.

Vague = scary.
Clear = certain.

**Process clarity answers:**

- What happens first?
- What happens next?
- What is required from me?
- How long does each step take?
- What will I receive at each stage?
- What if something goes wrong?

**For our company:**

- Step 1 — Dream Site Configurator
- Step 2 — Blueprint instantly
- Step 3 — FREE Strategy call with Wireframe & copy of their blueprint
- Step 4 — Build first draft
- Step 5 — Iterate & Present offers
- Step 6 — Launch
- Step 7 — Optimise

When people can see the path, they instantly trust the destination.

### 5. Personalisation

(The biggest secret PLA lever)

This is tailoring the experience to the buyer.

People trust what is made for them, not for "everyone."

**Examples:**

- Use their own words back to them
- Present solutions that match their exact scenario
- Break down their specific obstacles
- Show testimonials from people with that exact issue

Personalisation creates the feeling:
"This wasn't just made for someone.
This was made for me."

That skyrockets PLA.

---

## How PLA Influences Buying Behaviour

People don't buy the best offer.
They buy the offer they feel the most confidence in.

**High PLA removes:**

- hesitation
- doubt
- fear
- uncertainty
- procrastination

When PLA is high enough, the buyer often says:
"I just knew in my gut this was the right choice."

That gut feeling is PLA.

---

## The Simplest PLA Framework

To increase PLA, ask:
"What can I show, say, or prove that makes them believe
they will get the promised result — in their situation —
without stress or failure?"

If you answer this question well, the offer sells itself.

# 3. Time Delay

Time Delay is the perceived speed at which the customer believes they will get the promised result.

It answers the simple, powerful buying question:
"How fast will I get the thing I want?"

Human psychology is wired for immediacy.

The shorter the wait, the higher the perceived value.
The longer the wait, the lower the perceived value.

This is universally true across products and services.

---

## Why Time Delay Matters So Much

Because:
The shorter the delay,
the lower the friction,
the faster the dopamine,
the higher the perceived likelihood of achievement,
the easier the sale.

Time Delay doesn't just make something feel more valuable —
It actually increases the numerator of the value equation by lowering resistance in the denominator.

**Shorter =**

- More sales
- Higher urgency
- Higher conversions
- Less fear
- Less abandonment

**Longer =**

- Less excitement
- More worry
- More doubt
- Lower conversions

---

## There Are Two Types of Time Delay

### 1. Time Until Initial Result

"How soon will I see anything happen?"

This is the "first taste" of value — the fastest possible win.

**Examples:**

- Kawakawa balm → "Feel relief in minutes. Visible changes in hours."
- Weight loss → "Lose 1–2 kg in the first week."
- Website builds → "Your instant blueprint."

Initial result is the hook.

### 2. Time Until Final Result

"How long until I get the full transformation?"

This is the long-term or complete version of the outcome.

**Examples:**

- Kawakawa balm → "Full skin improvement in 7–14 days."
- Website build → "Full site live in 7–21 days."
- Fitness → "Body recomposition in 90 days."

Customers care about both —
but initial result matters more for urgency.

---

## The Four Levers That Reduce Time Delay

These are how you strategically shorten the perceived time delay.

### 1. Speed of Delivery

How fast until they receive something tangible?

**Products:**

- Fast shipping
- Same-day dispatch
- Digital confirmation
- Tracking updates
- Notification of batch preparation

**Services:**

- First deliverable quickly (blueprint, audit, scan)
- Book a call immediately
- Receive something in their inbox instantly

**Examples:**

- "Instant access."
- "Ships today."
- "Draft delivered within 48 hours."
- "You get your strategy within 24 hours of completing intake."

### 2. Speed of Relief / Speed of First Win

This is the "OMG I'm already getting value" moment.

**Products:**

- Balm → soothing sensation in minutes
- Supplements → energy within a day
- Apps → first task completed instantly

**Services:**

- First clarity within minutes
- First step feels easy
- Quick wins built into onboarding

This is one of the highest-converting levers.

### 3. Reduce Customer Input Time

"How long will I have to do stuff?"

If the customer feels they need to:

- write
- think
- gather
- research
- upload
- provide materials
- complete forms
- or "get around to it"

…then time delay expands dramatically.

Reducing their required effort shortens time delay.

**Examples:**

- "We do all the writing for you."
- "Just answer 10 simple questions."
- "No prep needed — we guide everything."
- "Upload or send nothing — we extract it all."

This is WHY your Dream Site Configurator is so powerful —
it reduces the perceived time delay of a website build by removing their workload.

### 4. Milestones & Visibility

When customers can see the process laid out clearly:

- The journey feels shorter
- The uncertainty disappears
- The end feels closer
- The trust goes up

**Examples:**

- A timeline on the sales page
- A dashboard with progress bars
- "Day 1 you get this. Day 3 you get this. Day 7 you get this."
- "Your order is in infusion."
- "Your website copy is drafting."
- "You're in batching stage."

Clarity = speed.

---

## The Psychology Behind Time Delay

People are willing to pay extra for:

- speed
- ease
- immediate relief
- faster clarity
- faster certainty
- faster transformation
- faster reassurance

A faster path is a more valuable path.

Time Delay has massive psychological weight because:
People don't buy the best option.
They buy the fastest option that still feels safe.

This is why Uber wins over taxis.
This is why instant downloads beat shipping.
This is why our instant website blueprint offer is so powerful.

This is why our instant blueprint is so powerful.

# 4. Effort & Sacrifice

Effort & Sacrifice is the perceived difficulty the customer believes they'll have to endure in order to get the promised result.

It answers the crucial question:
"What do I have to do, learn, give up, or endure to get this outcome?"

This includes:

- effort
- time
- emotion
- complexity
- lifestyle changes
- learning curves
- energy
- inconvenience
- willpower
- money
- risk
- discomfort

The simpler and smoother the path →
the higher the value.

The harder or more demanding →
the lower the value.

Even if the outcome is incredible,
if the effort feels too high, people hesitate.

---

## The Two Types of Effort & Sacrifice

### 1. Effort (Physical / Cognitive / Emotional Work)

This includes anything they must do:

- Fill out forms
- Write content
- Provide materials
- Perform tasks
- Learn something new
- Change habits
- Spend time
- Solve problems
- Communicate repeatedly
- Stay disciplined
- Figure out logistics
- Show up consistently

The more work required → the lower the perceived value.

### 2. Sacrifice (Giving Something Up)

This includes anything they must give up:

- Money
- Time with family
- Lifestyle comfort
- Old habits
- Favourite foods
- Freedom
- Convenience
- Emotional safety
- Control

If they feel like they're losing something important, value drops.

---

## Why Effort & Sacrifice Matter So Much

Because humans are wired to avoid:

- friction
- complexity
- confusion
- labor
- discomfort
- decision-making
- uncertainty
- responsibility

Even if the outcome is amazing,
if the path feels too demanding, they won't start.

This is the real reason behind procrastination.

---

## The 4 Drivers of High Effort & Sacrifice (what kills sales)

Understanding these helps you remove them.

### 1. Confusing Process

If the steps aren't clear, the brain predicts "This will be hard."

### 2. High Customer Involvement

When the customer must gather things, learn things, provide things, or constantly respond.

### 3. Lifestyle Disruption

If they need to give up comfort, habits, time, or convenience.

### 4. Fear of Failure / Effort Wasted

A hidden psychological sacrifice:
"What if I put in the effort and it doesn't work?"

High PLA reduces this, but effort magnifies it.

---

## The 4 Levers That Reduce Effort & Sacrifice (what increases value)

Effort is a perception before it's a reality.

You reduce it by adjusting how the path feels.

### 1. Done-For-You (DFY) — HIGHEST power lever

Removing work from the customer makes the value skyrocket.

**Examples:**

- "We write everything for you."
- "We build the website while you run your business."
- "We handle all the packaging and shipping."
- "We set up your automations."

People LOVE DFY because it removes cognitive load.

### 2. Done-With-You (DWY) — works when guidance reduces confusion

Even if customers must do something, if they feel supported, effort shrinks.

**Examples:**

- Step-by-step instructions
- Templates
- Prompts
- Scripts
- Checklists
- Co-building sessions
- Follow-along videos

DWY reduces mental friction.

### 3. Remove Decisions

Decision fatigue increases effort.

When you say:
"Choose any option you like,"
you create overwhelm.

Better:
"There are only 3 options — A, B, C."
"This one is best for your situation."
"Here's the recommended package."

Simplicity increases value.

### 4. Speed (reduces emotional effort)

A shorter path feels easier.

Long journeys feel draining before they start.

Time Delay & Effort reinforce each other.

---

## A Quick Example

**High Effort (low value):**
"To get your website built, please submit your logo, colours, images, writing, brand story, examples you like, and fill out these 12 forms."

**Low Effort (high value):**
"Just answer 12 easy questions in our Configurator — we handle everything from strategy to copy to design."

That sentence alone increases value dramatically.

---

**Another Example: Kawakawa Balm**

**High Effort (low value):**

**Low Effort (high value):**

Effort removed → value multiplied.
